The Trail Beyond Emberpeak

Chapter 1: Emberpeak's Edge

Talia Whitlock had grown up studying old maps and dreaming of the wild north. As the youngest cartographer in the Grand Guild, she was chosen to lead an expedition past Emberpeak—a volcanic ridge believed to mark the edge of the known world. But when her team sets out, they find the land is far more alive and hostile than expected: boiling rivers, shifting rocks, and glowing glyphs carved into mountain walls. One night, they’re attacked by creatures made of obsidian and ash.

Chapter 2: The Forgotten Depths

After losing most of her crew, Talia escapes into a chasm with only a wounded guide named Fen. Deep underground, they discover remnants of a civilization older than the continent—complete with living stone guardians and tunnels that rearrange themselves. Talia begins to have visions, guided by a voice calling itself Vaelor, the God of Earth. It tells her the maps were wrong; the land was never fully drawn because the world shifts with belief.

Chapter 3: The Living Map

To escape the depths and warn the world, Talia must finish a living map—an artifact that updates reality as it’s drawn. But the price is her memories. As she completes the last sketch, Fen sacrifices himself to buy her time. When she emerges back to the surface, Talia no longer remembers the journey, but her map pulses with light, shifting constantly. The Guild declares her mad, but her map draws wanderers from across the lands—each hoping to find truth beyond Emberpeak, where adventure writes itself in every step.
